The Brainy Gut

Interactive imagery visualizes the essence of the gut-brain axis connection through the movement of colonies. The system uses an "externalized gut," where changes in virtual colonies reflect the body's health status. The movement of the colonies is driven by the user's breath, with breathing patterns influencing the growth and transformation of the virtual gut, gradually mapping out its entire structure.

This not only activates the user's breath control but also influences the brain via neural signals, providing real-time feedback that triggers self-regulation mechanisms. As a result, players experience psychological and physiological benefits that affect both their gut and overall health.

Design principles

This technology combines micro-display screens with the refractive principles of lenses, encapsulating the internal components within the interlayer space of a spherical lens to achieve 3D display effects in compact devices. The micro-screen provides high-resolution images, while the lens focuses the refracted light into the user's eyes, creating a clear and immersive three dimensional visual experience.

Internal Structure

Visual Representation of Microbiota

Different types of virtual microbiota are modeled to exhibit various growthpatterns. For instance, some microbiota proliferate more rapidly in stressful environments, while their growth slows in healthy environments. These environmental changes can be simulated through the player's breathing depth, providing clear feedback and a sense of control.

Healthy microbiota are primarily yellow or blue, representing balance and harmony. However, when the player's breathing becomes shallow or anxious, some microbiota turn red, displaying chaotic or irregular movement, reflecting stress and discomfort.

Externalized Gut Setup

The virtual microbiota represents the dynamic changes of microorganisms within the gut, driven by the player's physiological responses, particularly breathing. By controlling their breathing frequency (e.g., slow or rapid), players influence the growth, reproduction, and simulation of the gut environment, thereby indirectly exploring the entire intestinal space.

The final display process offers an abstract representation of the interaction between the gut, breathing, and mental activity. The structure of the virtual gut evolves and adapts in real time, following the user's breath. The movement and changes of the virtual colonies reflect the indirect impact on respiratory health, providing a visual representation of the gut's response to different breathing patterns.

Over time, the system gradually unveils the full structure of the gut, creating an engaging and educational experience that links physiological processes with interactive imagery. The entire process ties together the relationship between the gut and brain through breath, making their invisible connection both visible and captivating.
